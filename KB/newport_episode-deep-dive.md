Deep Dive into HBO’s "The Gilded Age"
“Downton Abbey” creator Julian Fellowes chose the Newport Mansions to provide authentic scenery for his historical drama “
The Gilded Age
.” Scenes for the Emmy Award-winning series were filmed at
The Breakers
,
Marble House
,
The Elms
,
Rosecliff
,
Chateau-sur-Mer
,
Kingscote
and
Hunter House
.
Set in the 1880s, “The Gilded Age” tells the stories of “old money” New York elites and their escalating feud with nouveau riche neighbors who threaten the established social order.
Bertha Russell (Carrie Coon) and Ward McAllister (Nathan Lane) in the Ballroom of The Elms during Season 2 of “The Gilded Age” (Photograph by Barbara Nitke/HBO)
Do these places look familiar from the show?
Left: Consuelo Vanderbilt’s Bedroom in Marble House is George Russell’s bedroom. Right: The Billiard Room at The Breakers is the Russells’ billiard room in their New York mansion, where Mr. Russell and Alderman Morris play a game.
Left: The kitchen at The Elms is seen frequently as the Russells’ kitchen where their chef, butler and other servants work and gossip. Right: Also in The Elms, Mrs. Berwind’s Bedroom appears as the room of the Russells’ daughter, Gladys.
Left: The stairwell in Chateau-sur-Mer is seen as part of Mamie Fish’s New York house. Right: The dining room at Chateau-sur-Mer is where Mrs. Fish’s doll tea party is held. Elsewhere in Chateau-sur-Mer, the Butternut Bedroom is used for the apartment of man-about-town Oscar van Rhijn, while other rooms represent the bedrooms of Agnes van Rhijn, Alderman Morris and his wife, Anne.
Left: The white front façade of Rosecliff is striking as Mrs. Chamberlain’s house in Manhattan. Right: Chateau-sur-Mer is seen as the exterior of Mrs. Astor’s summer home in Newport.
Left: The climactic ballroom scene from the Season 1 finale. Right: The Breakers Music Room, where the scene was filmed.
Thanks to meticulous historical preservation, many of the Gilded Age mansions in Newport remain standing — and even served as filming locations for the HBO show.

Inspiration from the Newport Mansions
Spaces and features in the Newport Mansions directly inspired the sets created by “The Gilded Age” Production Designer Bob Shaw. Bertha Russell’s room is modeled after
Alva Vanderbilt’s bedroom in Marble House
, while the
Marble House grand staircase
with its ornate wrought-iron railing influenced its counterpart in the Russells’ mansion.
“The Gilded Age” also draws upon the history and real-life people of the period. The parallels between the character Bertha Russell and
Alva Vanderbilt of Marble House
are striking. Alva famously manipulated the queen of New York society, Caroline Schermerhorn Astor, into attending her gala costume ball by refusing to invite Mrs. Astor’s daughter unless Mrs. Astor called upon her. Mrs. Astor called; the Astors then attended the Vanderbilt ball; and Alva secured her place in society. This true story is lifted from history in “The Gilded Age,” where Bertha Russell uses the same strategy.
