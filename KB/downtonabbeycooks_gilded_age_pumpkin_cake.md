# Taste the Tradition: Pumpkin Cake from The Gilded Age Cookbook
Source: https://www.downtonabbeycooks.com/taste-the-tradition-pumpkin-cake-from-the-gilded-age-cookbook/
Author: Pamela Foster (Downton Abbey Cooks)
Recipe from: The Gilded Age Cookbook by Becky Libourel Diamond

## Historical Context

During the Gilded Age, pumpkin extended beyond traditional pie applications. The era showcased innovative approaches to cooking, with squashes finding their way into various desserts. Notably, "there are no traditional pumpkin spices in this dish," distinguishing it from modern pumpkin desserts.

The recipe represents how the Gilded Age approached seasonal ingredients creatively, offering "warmth and nostalgia" while connecting modern palates with historical culinary practices.

## Gilded Age Pumpkin Cake Recipe

**Yields:** 16 servings

### Cake Ingredients
- ½ cup softened unsalted butter
- 1 cup granulated sugar
- 1 cup packed brown sugar
- 2 beaten large eggs
- 1 cup pumpkin puree
- 3 cups cake flour
- 4 teaspoons baking powder
- ¼ teaspoon baking soda
- ½ cup milk
- 1 cup chopped walnuts
- 1 teaspoon maple extract

### Maple Butter Frosting
- 6 tablespoons softened unsalted butter
- 3½ cups confectioners sugar
- 3 tablespoons milk
- ½ teaspoon vanilla extract

### Method
Preheat oven to 350°F. Prepare two greased and floured 9-inch round cake pans. Cream butter with sugars, incorporate eggs and pumpkin, then alternate adding sifted dry ingredients with milk. Fold in walnuts and maple extract. Bake 30–40 minutes until set. Cool briefly before removing from pans. Frost with maple butter frosting once completely cooled.
